Intranet vs. Internet

An Intranet is a private network that only established members of an organization can access. These are typically unavailable to the general populace and has strict access controls.

The Internet works a lot like an intranet but without the access controls. This means that internet sites can be accessed by anyone, although some access controls can still be used to restrict features to user's who have accounts for that website.

Uses of Intranet vs. Internet

Businesses will typically use an Intranet to give their employees the resources they need to succeed in their business. This can mean providing them with tools they need on a daily basis, access employee benefits, communicate with one another, share information to the business and filter only company news, among many other uses.

Businesses will also use the internet to directly interact with their customers. This could mean providing their users with specific information on their products, give them access to manage their accounts, provide web applications to interface with their products directly and many more uses.
